Thryve · Product Owner

Hired to check the work. Built the machine that checks it better.

Thryve hired me as employee #7 — as a QA analyst, not a PM. Handed manual test evaluation, I built my own Excel tooling for test runs and data-model mapping, and wrote requirements tickets strong enough to stand out. Eight months later I was Product Owner.

The same instinct scaled with the company: personal Python automation kept product, ops, QA, and partner integration moving while the team grew from 7 to 35 — and I turned my overlapping-hats knowledge into Thryve Academy, a self-recorded video library plus dual-audience release notes that onboarded every team.

  • Promoted QA analyst → Product Owner in 8 months, after building my own QA tooling and standout requirements tickets.
  • Scaled the integration backbone to 1M+ end users, 100+ B2B partners, and 10+ wearable and health-app integrations — while the company grew from €0 to €1M ARR.
  • Built Thryve Academy — self-recorded videos plus dual-audience release notes — as the team grew from 7 to 35.

LabTwin · Technical Product Manager

The machine here was a retraining cadence.

I joined LabTwin weeks after ChatGPT launched — a deliberate bet on AI. When generative AI rewrote the field mid-flight, I spearheaded migrating intent recognition to GPT-3.5, shipping an LLM into a production voice pipeline for pharma and biotech customers in 2023.

The pattern showed up in the upkeep: I ran the speech retraining program with the data scientists, feeding customer-specific glossaries and real voice data back into the models — 95% scientific-term accuracy for key customers — and designed the retraining cadence to cut its cost.

  • Shipped GPT-3.5 intent recognition into a production voice pipeline in 2023.
  • Drove speech recognition to 95% scientific-term accuracy for key customers, with a retraining cadence designed to cut its cost.
  • Tripled deployment frequency from 4 to 12 releases per year.

Labforward · Senior Product Manager

Validation before commitment.

At Labforward, I owned the full GenAI roadmap and changed how ideas moved from request to build. My operating principle was simple: validate before committing engineering time.

The machine I built was a rapid-prototyping loop: a prototype in about 4 hours, validated in about 1 day, replacing design cycles that could run 2-12 weeks before the team learned what users actually meant.

The result was tighter product judgement, faster validation, and AI work connected to the daily lab workflows users already cared about.

  • Reduced time-to-validation from 8-12 weeks to 2-3 weeks.
  • Cut rework from 50% to 15%.
  • Raised feature adoption from 40% to 75% and supported a €500K contract renewal.

Sema · Senior AI Product Manager

From read-only analytics to a colleague that acts.

At Sema I lead product on Liz, an AI agent for software-delivery management. The machine-building here is the product itself: I turned Liz from read-only analytics into a colleague that acts inside the org’s own tools — bi-directional Slack chat, governed Jira write-back, Confluence read and write — shipped to production, resting on the integration layer I designed across Jira, GitHub, Slack, Zoom, Linear, email, Teams, and documents.

And I govern the machine’s own cost: cut daily AI spend ~39% through data-driven cost governance — continuous monitoring of per-insight usage and spend, re-run cadences redesigned around real demand — without reducing customer coverage.

  • Turned Liz from read-only analytics into an agent that takes governed actions in Slack, Jira, and Confluence — in production.
  • Designed the integration layer feeding Liz across the org's core work systems.
  • Cut daily AI spend ~39% through data-driven cost governance, preserving customer coverage.
